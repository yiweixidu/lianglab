Research frontiers: from molecular locks to AI‑driven discovery

HIV-1 & host restriction

Deciphering how IFITM proteins, MxB, APOBEC3G, and tetherin block HIV-1 at entry, reverse transcription, and release. Using proteomic screens to uncover novel restriction factors.

CRISPR‑based HIV cure

Pioneered application of CRISPR-Cas9 to excise integrated HIV-1 provirus from infected cells. Current work: precision delivery, escape mutations & combination with latency reversal.

SARS-CoV-2 & interferon dysregulation

Understanding aberrant innate immune responses in severe COVID-19; discovering small molecules that inhibit coronavirus replication (e.g., anthracyclines).

AI + multi‑omics integration

Applying machine learning to genomic/proteomic datasets to predict viral‑host interactions, accelerate drug repurposing, and design RNA‑targeting molecules.

Why Liang Lab in the AI era?

🤖 AI‑augmented virology

We combine high‑throughput CRISPR screens, single‑cell transcriptomics, and structural biology with generative AI models to predict viral escape and design novel antivirals. The lab is actively building AI agents that mine viral evolution data and propose experimental hypotheses.

We are seeking computational biologists, machine learning engineers, and physicists who are eager to tackle pandemic threats. Cross‑disciplinary training is at our core — wet lab and dry lab work side by side.
